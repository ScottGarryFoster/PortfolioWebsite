ETHICS

Most people have a concept of ethics in their day to lives whether they be political, moral, or environmental. When considering how I would like to approach my career there are certain aspects of the profession which I could only consider my ethical decisions as a software engineer. These guiding principles are the basis for which the decision I make should stand upon for me to consider the work I produce to be ‘ethical’ at least in my own. These are largely drawn from works in the field from established engineers.

As professed by Robert Martin, software engineers rule the world, or rather other people make the rules, and we program those rules. There isn’t much of anything you can do in this society which does not involve a piece of software.

This page contains the main ethical pillars I stand by and what they mean to me to ensure my work remains at a high (and ethically just) standard.

Articles

Main Goals: Education and Growth

My blog attempts to use researched methodologies paired with well known technologies to give solid advice for reader who may be looking to take their game project to the next level. I write about clean code, test driven development and design discipline. The posts are written with one or two pieces of research (generally books) in mind to ensure that the advice given is done so in good faith and where opinions (or punditry) are given they are expressed openly. The content is given as a jumping off point with sources given.

Test driven development is a theme among these blog posts generally because in the gaming material models (such as mocking, dependency injection, seams) are used without consideration for the technique (in a lot of tutorials). When approaching any model or technique the importance should be on why the model is used, it’s advantages/disadvantages and the technique itself. Given the material researched before I began writing appeared to lack in this area, I felt my blog had something to offer.

General themes of the blog are to be the pillars I stand by for software development, clean, confident, and robust code. Design and documentation also do appear but these lean more heavily on growth and research.
